Katsu Curry

Description

Katsu Curry is a Japanese dish that consists of breaded chicken, rice and a curry sauce made out of vegetables and katsu sauce.

There is a moderate amount of prep-work required, but if you're in a pinch consider cooking the chicken in the oven instead of frying. This dish should comfortably serve 3 or 4 people.

Ingredients

  • 2 Chicken Breasts (Medium-Sized)
  • 3 Carrots (Medium-Sized)
  • 4 Yellow Potatoes (Medium-Sized)
  • Rice (1.5 cups, uncooked)
  • 3 whole eggs
  • Flour (12 tablespoons)
  • Panko Breadcrumbs (12 tablespoons)
  • 3 Katsu Curry stock cubes
  • Salt, Pepper, Olive Oil

Steps

  1. Begin boiling water for vegetables.
  2. Wash rice in rice cooker and then start rice cooker with appropriate amount of water.
  3. Cut carrots and potatoes into a medium-large dice of approximately equal size.
  4. Boil all vegetables with water in a pot until they have become more tender, but not soft. Add a pinch of salt to the vegetables.
  5. Prepare breading station by beating eggs in a bowl, placing mixture of flour, a pinch of salt and a crack of pepper on a plate and placing Panko breadcrumbs on a second plate.
  6. Cut chicken breast in half to create a larger flat surface area to use for the dish. A flatter shape will prevent chicken being undercooked while being fried.
  7. Dry the flat chicken breasts in flour, then submerge in the egg wash before finally coating them in breadcrumbs.
  8. Shallow fry the breaded chicken in olive oil in a frypan. Continue frying until chicken is crisp and golden brown.
  9. While the chicken fries, drain out 95% of the vegetable water and stir in 2 of the curry stock cubes.
  10. Stir curry and vegetable mixture well until sauce reaches appropriate consistency - it should be viscous but not thick. Add water and the final stock cube as appropriate to reach this consistency.
  11. Slice up fried chicken breast in the style of your choosing to make consumption easier.
  12. Arrange breaded chicken, rice and curry sauce on a plate and then serve.
